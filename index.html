Scroll down more to know more about me!

Why computer science AND chemistry? I initially chose to major in chemistry because I was interested in working in the hardware tech industry, such as making batteries, screens, and cameras. But after being a student in Berkeley for over a year, I was influenced by the huge wave of Computer Science students and Bay Area tech companies. I fast tracked my CS degree and took all the requirements to declare Computer Science BA major in just two semesters. I had the intent to switch out of the chemistry major, but realized that I could use my software to solve my difficult chemistry problems and understandings. After taking all the lower division requirements needed for a computer science major in two semesters, my perception of the world changed, and I saw software as the answer to all things. Every time I interact with technology, in the back of my head, I am always thinking how this piece of hardware or software is programmed to interact with me. I plan to expand my computer science field with chemistry by finishing my Chemistry BS degree off with a concentration in computational chemistry and quantum computing. My interests career wise today is software engineering. And I have interest in artificial intelligence and quantum computing. Hopefully, the rise of quantum computer will come near and we can change how we use computer hardware in the future.
